About Me

Hey there! I’m Asmi, a Computer Science student with a growing passion for data analytics. What started as curiosity quickly turned into a genuine interest in working with data—cleaning it up, analyzing trends, and visualizing insights in a way that actually makes sense. I love using tools like Python, SQL, Excel, and Power BI to dive into datasets and uncover patterns that tell a story. There's something really exciting about solving real-world problems using data, whether it's through a dashboard, a statistical model, or just a well-structured report.

This portfolio is a collection of some of my favorite projects—academic assignments, personal experiments, and everything in between. Each one has taught me something new and pushed me to think more critically about how we use data to make decisions. I'm always exploring new tools, techniques, and ideas, and I see this space as a reflection of that ongoing learning journey. Thanks for checking it out, and I hope you enjoy browsing through my work!

Digital Village – Smarter Irrigation Through Data

As a Remote Sensing Intern with Tel Aviv University, Israel, I’m contributing to a large-scale project using satellite data and analytics to optimize irrigation in Punjab. By analyzing evapotranspiration across a sample from over a 1000 plots, we're identifying sustainable farming practices that reduce water waste.
